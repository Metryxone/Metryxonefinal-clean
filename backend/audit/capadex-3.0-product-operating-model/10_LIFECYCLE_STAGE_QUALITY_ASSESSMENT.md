# 10 · Lifecycle Stage Quality Assessment

Scores the five stages against the brief's quality criteria: Clearly-Defined · Measurable · Universal ·
Practical · Scalable. (1–5; honest, repo-evidenced.)

| Quality criterion | Awareness | Curiosity | Clarity | Growth | Mastery | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Clearly defined** | 2 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 5 | Awareness is implicit; others have explicit purpose + activities. |
| **Measurable** | 1 | 4 | 4 | 3 | 4 | Stages carry weights & assessments, but advancement isn't score-gated. |
| **Universal** | 4 | 5 | 5 | 5 | 5 | Same taxonomy applies across all personas — strong. |
| **Practical** | 3 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 | Each maps to a concrete product/session; Growth's re-test is the soft spot. |
| **Scalable** | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | Compute-on-demand (no per-stage persistence bottleneck); load unproven. |

## Quality findings
- **Curiosity & Clarity are the strongest stages** (well-defined, assessed, AI-interpreted, reported).
- **Awareness is the weakest** — it is an implicit pre-state, not a managed stage. This is defensible (it's
  pre-assessment) but means the funnel's top is unmeasured.
- **Growth's measurability gap is the highest-leverage quality issue:** it prescribes action (30-day plan) but
  does not *measure* whether growth occurred (no mandatory re-assessment) → the stage cannot prove its own
  success metric. (→ GAP-P1)
- **Measurability is the lowest-scoring criterion overall** — directly traceable to the absence of hard
  entry/exit criteria and the monetization-gated (not evidence-gated) progression model.

## Verdict
**Stage quality: GOOD for definition & universality; WEAK for measurability & progression.** The stages are a
solid skeleton; the missing muscle is *evidence-gated movement and measured growth*. Enhancement-only.
