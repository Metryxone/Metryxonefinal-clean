## The verdict in one paragraph

MetryxOne Competency Assessment is a **structurally excellent, scientifically well-architected engine in a pre-validation, cold-start state.** The science (IRT 3PL/EAP, Cronbach α, Bayesian mastery, 4/5ths adverse impact, reliability engine), the 12-layer ontology, the explainable traceable recommendations, and the k-anonymous benchmarking are **genuinely strong by design — arguably best-in-class architecture for an emerging player.** But the live database is nearly empty where it matters (curated question bank = 0, O*NET ontology not imported, EI scores = 0, benchmarks below k=30, **zero realised outcomes, zero sales**). **You have built world-class machinery; you have not yet proven it with data.**

**Final classification:** **Beta Ready** as a product · **Pilot Ready** for Institutions (concierge) and gated Employer use · **Not Yet** world-class · **Not Ready** as a scientific instrument or enterprise product.

---

## The 5 decisions you need to make

1. **Go-to-market: lead with Institutions (concierge B2B2C) + gated Employer pilots.** These are credible *today* on structural strength and honest framing. Do **not** launch self-serve student/professional commercial or enterprise yet.
2. **Treat the first pilots as a validation engine, not just revenue.** Their job is to manufacture the two missing assets — **populated live data** and **realised outcomes**. Instrument outcome capture from day one.
3. **Hold the line on disallowed claims.** No predictive, hiring, promotion, or "world-class/validated" claims until you have published reliability/validity and ≥1 outcome correlation. Your codebase already enforces this (disallowed AI verdicts) — keep it.
4. **Fund activation over features.** The highest-leverage work is *not* more engines — it's importing O*NET, populating/calibrating the bank, activating EI, isolating prod DB. (See Stage 0 of the roadmap.)
5. **Fix the two operational landmines before any paid pilot:** crisis detection has **no human-notify path** (safety), and email (Zoho) is a **single point of failure** for MFA/OTP (lockout). Both are small fixes with outsized risk.
