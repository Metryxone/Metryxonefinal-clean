# Section 17 — Enterprise Maturity Assessment

**Scoring lens:** each axis blends Architecture (built), Activation (fed/reachable), and Usage/Outcomes
(real users + realized results). Because the platform's *purpose* is outcomes and outcomes are
near-zero, scores are deliberately held below their architecture-only level. **No score is inflated by
demo data or unproven accuracy.** Target = 98%+ (enterprise-certified, real-usage validated).

## 17.2 Overall maturity

> **Scoring method.** Per-axis scores are honest blends of Architecture/Activation/Usage. The
> *unweighted arithmetic mean* of the 15 axes is ≈ **53.7%**. The headline **≈ 52%** is *outcome-
> weighted*: axes that represent realized value (Validation, Usage-bearing domains — Employer, Career
> Builder, Workforce, Experience, Analytics) are weighted slightly higher than pure-infrastructure
> axes (Architecture, O*NET content), pulling the blend down ~1.7 points because the outcome-bearing
> axes score lowest. Either figure (≈52 outcome-weighted, ≈54 unweighted) supports the same
> conclusion; the precise number is not load-bearing.

- **Current overall (honest, outcome-weighted): ≈ 52%** (unweighted mean ≈ 54%).
- **Architecture-only would read ≈ 85%** — the 33-point delta *is* the activation+usage gap. This
  delta is the single most important number in the certification: **the platform is built far ahead of
  where it is used.**
- **Target: 98%+.**

## 17.3 Gap analysis (what the 46 points are made of)
1. **Content (≈ the largest share):** question→competency mapping (1.7% → broad), graded multi-
   difficulty question bank, curated Role DNA depth, region-conditioned competency expectations.
2. **Usage:** real users completing assessments and career journeys (today: 2 users, 0 completed
   sessions); multi-org employer/workforce activation (today: 1 demo org).
3. **Outcomes:** realized outcomes flowing into the Validation Loop (today: 0) — the only thing that
   unlocks any accuracy/calibration claim across employer, workforce, and predictive surfaces.
4. **Operational governance & commercial security under real traffic** (today: armed, unexercised).

## 17.4 Honest framing for the founder
MetryxOne is a **high-maturity architecture at a low-maturity activation/outcome stage.** The gap to
98% is **overwhelmingly data, usage, and realized outcomes — not engineering.** That is a far better
position than the reverse, and it makes the roadmap (Section 18) a *population and go-to-market*
exercise more than a build exercise.
