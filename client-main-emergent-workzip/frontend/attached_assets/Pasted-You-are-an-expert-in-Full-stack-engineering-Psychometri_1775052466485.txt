## 🎯 3.1 MULTI-METHOD ASSESSMENT DESIGN

Each competency must be measured using **at least 2 methods**:

---

### 1. Objective Tests (MCQ / Numeric)

Used for:

* Numerical aptitude
* Logical reasoning
* Analytical thinking

Structure:

* question_id
* difficulty_level (easy/medium/hard)
* correct_answer
* time_taken

---

---

### 2. Situational Judgment Tests (SJT)

Used for:

* Decision making
* Ethics
* leadership
* interpersonal skills

Structure:

* scenario
* 4–5 options
* scoring weight per option

---

---

### 3. Self-Assessment (Likert Scale)

Used for:

* behavioral traits
* motivation
* emotional intelligence

Scale:
1–5 (Strongly Disagree → Strongly Agree)

---

---

### 4. Behavioral Simulation (Rule-Based)

Used for:

* problem solving
* prioritization
* execution

Example:

* “You have 5 tasks, limited time—what do you do?”

Score based on:

* priority correctness
* decision path

---

---

### 5. Writing Assessment

Used for:

* communication
* clarity

Evaluate using:

* keyword matching
* sentence structure (basic NLP)

# 🔷 4. SCORING MODEL (PRACTICAL)

---

## 🎯 Step 1: Question-Level Score

MCQ:
Score = 1 if correct else 0

---

SJT:
Score = predefined weight (0–1)

---

Likert:
Normalize to 0–1

---

Simulation:
Score based on rule-based evaluation

---

---

## 🎯 Step 2: Competency Score

Competency Score =
Σ(question_score × weight) / total_weight

---

---

## 🎯 Step 3: Multi-Source Aggregation

Final Competency Score =
(Assessment Score × 0.5)

* (CV Score × 0.2)
* (Experience Score × 0.2)
* (Behavior Score × 0.1)

---

---

## 🎯 Step 4: Confidence Score

Based on:

* number of questions attempted
* consistency
* data sources

---

Final Score =
Competency Score × Confidence
